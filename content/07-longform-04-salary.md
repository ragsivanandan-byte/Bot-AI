### SECTION 1 — You're Taxed in the Wrong Order (0:30–2:15)
**[VISUAL]** Fiche de paie élégante, montant qui rétrécit
**VOICE:**
> Start with the order of operations, because this is the hidden trap. As an employee, here's your sequence: you earn, the government taxes you *first*, and you live on whatever's left. Earn, get taxed, then spend. You never even touch a big chunk of what you earned.
>
> **[B-ROLL]** Flux d'argent : gros montant → impôt → petit reste
> Now watch how the wealthy structure it. They own assets and businesses. The business earns, it spends on legitimate expenses *first*, and it's taxed on what's left — *after*. Earn, spend, then get taxed on the remainder. Same economy, opposite order. That ordering alone can mean keeping far more of every dollar.
>
> **[VISUAL]** Deux escaliers : "employee" vs "owner", or/noir
> This is why a high salary feels like running on a treadmill. The system is designed to tax employment heavily and immediately, because it's the easiest income to track. A salary isn't a path to wealth — it's the most taxed, least flexible way to make money there is. It's fuel. It is not the engine.

---

### SECTION 2 — Trading Time for Money Has a Ceiling (2:15–4:00)
**[VISUAL]** Horloge + pièces, concept temps = argent
**VOICE:**
> Here's the second wall. A salary trades your *time* for money — and time is the one thing you can't make more of. There are only so many hours in a week. Even if you get the raise, the promotion, the bonus, you're still capped by the hours you can physically work. Double your salary and you've still got the same twenty-four hours, now with more stress attached.
>
> **[B-ROLL]** Personne au bureau tard le soir, fatigue, ville qui dort
> The wealthy escape this by building income that *doesn't* require their time. A book that sells while they sleep. A business with employees. Shares of a company that grows whether they show up or not. Rent from a property. This is the difference between *active* income — you work, you get paid, you stop, it stops — and *passive* income, which keeps flowing after the work is done.
>
> **[VISUAL]** Une source d'eau vs plusieurs sources, abondance
> A salary is a single pipe, and you have to keep pumping it by hand. Wealth is built when you install pipes that flow on their own. The goal isn't a bigger salary. It's replacing the need to pump at all.

---

### SECTION 3 — Assets vs Liabilities: The Real Divide (4:00–6:00)
**[VISUAL]** Balance : actif qui rapporte vs passif qui coûte
**VOICE:**
> So if not the salary, what *does* make you rich? One thing: owning assets. And most people get this backwards, because they confuse looking rich with being rich.
>
> **[ON-SCREEN]** "Asset = puts money IN. Liability = takes money OUT."
> Here's the only definition you need. An asset puts money *into* your pocket. A liability takes money *out*. That's it. Stocks that pay dividends, a rental property, a business, a fund that grows — assets. The financed luxury car, the watch on credit, the bigger apartment you can barely afford — liabilities. They *look* like success and quietly drain you every month.
>
> **[B-ROLL]** Voiture de luxe vs immeuble locatif, comparaison
> The middle-class script is: get a raise, buy a nicer liability, repeat. The salary goes up, but so do the things eating it, so you never actually get ahead. The wealthy script is: take income, buy *assets*, let the assets pay for the lifestyle. They buy the liability only *after* an asset is paying for it.
>
> **[VISUAL]** Petit actif → flux → grand actif, effet boule de neige
> And assets compound. A dollar invested in a good asset can become many dollars over time without you doing anything more. A dollar spent on a liability is just… gone, plus the monthly cost it drags behind it. Wealth is simply the gap between what you *own* that pays you, and what you *owe* that costs you.

### SECTION 4 — How to Actually Start (6:00–7:15)
**[VISUAL]** Bureau minimaliste, carnet, café, plan
**VOICE:**
> So what do you actually *do* — especially if all you have right now is a salary? Three moves.
>
> **[ON-SCREEN]** "1. Use the salary as fuel"
> One. Stop expecting the salary to make you rich, and start using it as *fuel* for buying assets. Every paycheck, a fixed slice goes into assets *before* you spend a cent on lifestyle. Pay your future self first.
>
> **[ON-SCREEN]** "2. Widen the gap"
> Two. Wealth is the gap between earning and spending. You widen it from both sides: earn more *and* refuse to inflate your lifestyle every time you do. The person who keeps their costs flat while income rises gets rich quietly and fast.
>
> **[ON-SCREEN]** "3. Buy assets relentlessly"
> Three. Put that gap into assets, again and again, and let compounding do the heavy lifting. Boring, consistent, automatic. That's the whole game. Not the salary — what you do *with* it.
